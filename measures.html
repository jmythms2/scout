Measure Database

View, edit, and add measures to the database of next-generation building products.

This is currently an incomplete product. The measures shown are for testing purposes and do not represent any commercialized, pre-commercial, real, or imaginary products, programs, devices, or systems. Many elements on this page are non-functional.

Measures are specific technologies or products that are more efficient than the technology or product currently used in buildings to provide the same building service. For example, lighting is an important building service. A (hypothetical) new type of light bulb might provide 200 lumens (a measure of visible light) per Watt (denoted lm/W). To evaluate the energy savings possible from the sales and installation of this new light bulb, it must first be defined with a measure. The measure for the light bulb will include information about efficiency, price, how long the bulb lasts, and other key attributes.

In the table of measures below, a summary of key parameters defining each measure is shown. Additional details can be viewed by clicking on an individual measure.

Before adding a measure to the database, please review the section in the Wiki on the attributes of a well-posed measure.
